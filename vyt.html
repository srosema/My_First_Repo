Research Objectives
Charitable donation habits of individuals who support the arts.
Goals, needs, and pains points donating, signing up for classes and purchasing tickets.
Our team chose a non-profit that has, like so many other organizations that rely on bringing people together for an experience, been hit particularly hard during the pandemic. We felt that their website, while functional and generally usable, still had room for improvement. Some user flows, most notably some involving revenue-generating links, were hard to figure out. To that end we wanted to make sure any services they were still able to provide (i.e. online classes and direct donations) had an improved user flow.

To further analyze of the current site we conducted a Heuristic Evaluation. This evaluation process confirmed what we already were seeing at first glance: the website is generally functional but could use a few usability tweaks. Seeing where the original site was weak and where it was strong guided us toward what was important for our design.
After interviewing our stakeholder contact, we found out some of the Valley Youth Theatre’s competitors. During this analysis process, we discovered that while VYT’s website had room for improvement, it was already one of the better youth theatre websites we came across. We learned a handful of “do’s” from these competitors and even more “do not’s”
Problem Statement
A busy parent feels frustrated about getting her kids involved in theater classes and needs to be able to quickly find information to sign up for classes but faces a challenge of a confusing website layout.
With a Value Proportion, visualizing the actual value your design can bring to the user is possible. Every additional time we use these in UX research, it becomes more and more evident exactly why they’re useful and how they contribute to the final product.
What can a little bit of data do? We saw our persona go from a sketched out idea of a person to someone who felt like a real user just by incorporating some interview and survey responses. Rebecca went from 2D to 3D just like that.
Proto Persona
User Persona
Once we fleshed out Rebecca’s persona, we were able to thoroughly reflect on her direct user experience with the website. In this storyboard, we picture a scenario that has her using the website in a way that would serve her needs best.
Rebecca is looking for an extracurricular activity for her two children. She is a busy parent with a career and a family that take most of her attention and time.

Her kids had expressed an interest in acting and the theater. She is a supporter of the arts and thinks that expanding her children's experience in this area is a great idea.
Rebecca easily finds information for beginners, classes, tickets, and even how to donate. She signs up her children for some beginning classes to get them started. and also purchases some tickets for the whole family for the next performance
She has tried searching the web for a children's theaters in her are but has been frustrated by their websites and the lack of information for families just starting out.
She and her kids are excited and looking forward to being able to participate at Valley Youth Theater.
A friend tells her about Valley Youth Theater. She goes to their website. She immediately finds all the information she is looking for.
She soon finds herself inviting relatives and friends to the next performance with her children on stage. She is proud of their accomplishments and growth.

Theatre, children’s theatre in particular, is meant to be fun! We want first and foremost for the website to reflect that by immediately grabbing attention. But on the other hand there’s no fun to be had if you can’t find what you need, so clear usability is of course also extremely high priority. So, what we need is a website that immediately communicates the magic of theatre while also helping the users experience it.

Key Learnings
Making contact with a stakeholder was crucial for the redesign because it emphasized the importance of understanding the specific needs of your users.
It felt impactful to work on a redesign for a local non-profit and made the whole project feel more purposeful - we’re designing solutions for real life problems & people.
