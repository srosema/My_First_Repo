Stakeholder Interview
Madolyn Whitmer - Director of Education & Outreach

1) What are the main goals of your non-profit?
Bring in children from all walks of life to experience the performing arts and all the benefits of that, build confidence, feel value, and find a community.
Do this in a variety of ways - through shows that students can participate for free and make sure that every student is able to participate in our shows.
Educational programming - Classes take place throughout the week.
Several initiatives to give back to the community including, Literacy and arts program - bring student to the theatre for a free field trip experience, watch the show, get a book, and study guide (educational component).

2) Who is your primary audience?
Parents - seeing if classes are available, what plays are out there
Donors - grants & donations from the community
Customers - attending the shows

3) What are some goals your non-profit hopes to accomplish in the next year to five years?
Hard to say now because of COVID
Want to move forward and have a dream to have a new and larger facility that would bring in more people, be able to grow our mission
Bring VYT to as many children as possible
Increase their reach

4) Are there any frequency asked questions you receive that may or may not already be addressed somewhere on the website?
A lot of opportunities for user friendly things
A lot of questions received are already on the website
Doesn’t often get questions for things not on the website
Classes, schedules
Hard to figure out how to sign up for classes on the site
Parents don’t have a lot of time to figure out what to click on and where to find information

5) What do you like about the website? What do you not like?
There is so much - which is fair because they do a lot
There are central things that should be highlighted
Current main source of revenue is classes due to COVID
Takes a while to get to Classes
Usability to highlight main things
Donors is important too but doesn’t feel like they go through the website often
Don’t know how to access auditions and audition forms

6) What is your "website wishlist"?
Some kind of usability
Thinks it’s really difficult for someone to find Winter Classes, for instance, and feels like it isn’t very clear what to do from there
Pages were built and modified off of a basic template
The potential to have a “Start Here” section to walk through what they need to do and where they need to go - easy start guide

7) How do most parents contact your organization to get signed up? Do they go through the website primarily?
Lot of places where they can contact her for Education
Gets emails about classes, redirects them back to the site where all that info is at
Receives a lot of emails through the box office, confusion around education and auditions
Mostly email inquiries

8) How does your non-profit solicit donations?
Have campaigns done throughout the year
Special events
VYTal Affair (main fundraising event)
End of the Year Campaign
Call to Actions at the start of the year to ask people to be donors throughout the year for a certain dollar amount
Lots of grant works
Show sponsorships
Feels it is difficult to procure donors from a website
Think focusing on the mission, videos and images of children enjoying the programming would be more impactful
Website is part of the marketing mix, part of the pie, needs to be doing it’s job

9) Who do you consider your direct competitors for your donation dollars?
Child’s Play
Grease Paint
East Valley
All the folks who are in the children’s theatre business near us

Other notes
All donors are currently being showcased on the homepage because of an ongoing initiative, it isn’t typically like this
Callboard - announcements of casting, who is being picked to play in what productions
Parents at 4pm, have five minutes to get registration in - notes for persona
Ticketing site that they run through for classes, tickets, and donations needs a lot of work too
A lot of the audience are senior citizens, grandparents, older members of the community - the more user friendly, the more impactful
Potential user testing tasks:
Ticketing
Signing up for classes
Donating
Difference between classes & auditions?
Classes
Learning about the arts
Identify if they have a passion for it
More process oriented
Hone one’s skills
Have audition-style classes
Inclusion, find your spot
Fan the flame for the performing arts
Not meant to be a pipeline for the auditions
Auditions
Always open call
Anyone in a class can do an audition, but not guaranteed to get into the shows if enrolled
