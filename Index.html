People often feel overwhelmed when taking on the challenge of rearranging their room.  With setbacks like cleaning, measuring, and actually moving the items can all add to an unenjoyable experience.      
To see how people go about rearranging their living spaces.
To understand their precautions, processes, and pain points while completing this arduous task.
Users were asked questions that required them to explain their processes for tasks related to rearranging their living spaces.
"Why are you arranging your spaces?"
"What are the biggest pain points while rearranging a room?"
"What do you take into consideration when rearranging your space?"

With their responses we were able to find common struggles and areas  of focus and improvement were classified.
"I usually rearrange to accommodate new furniture."
"I make sure to keep in mind where the outlets are!"
"The flow and visual appeal of the room are my primary concerns"

With multiple software already existing that can make very detailed floor plans, MakeRoom aims to simplify complex features and remove all the unnecessary options and processes to allow the user to focus on the main task at hand.
Simplify existing software so it was usable by everyone with a balanmce between simplicity and versatility.
Since our idea wasn’t something new we were able to use existing software and choose what we thought would be best for the user.  We wanted to work around a grid system to keep everything consistent and organized, so the sketching phase was very easy and in turn made brainstorming more productive.
Objective
To find areas of confusion within the flow of the app.

Tasks
Users were asked to create a new account and complete the following tasks.
1. Create a New Room
2. Complete the Layout by adding fixtures to the room
3. Begin populating new room with furniture

Success Criteria
By the end of the test users should end on a screen with everything they did in front of them.  A floor plan with accurate fixtures, openings, and moveable furnishings.
All the users who participated were able to complete the tasks.

These were some of the common complaints that came up

“I didn’t know what was a button and what wasn’t.”

“Too many screens, I just want to see my floor plan.”

“Will I be able to see the dimensions?”

“I can’t delete any of these outlets?”
Make Room is driven by the idea that moving your belongings, whether new or existing, can be simplified by creating a visualization of your layout before the labor.

Creating a walk through video for new users to learn how to use the tools that are essential for the app.

With the simple idea that is driving the app, almost anyone should be able to pick it up and use it.
